# 🐭 Mouse Orchestra
**isatma.org · Educational & Developmental · v6 · April 17, 2025**

---

## What Is It?

Mouse Orchestra is a gentle, browser-based interactive music experience designed for children. A circle of colorful mice each play a musical note when hovered over. The center mouse spins and glows with each note played. Children can explore sound, melody, and music at their own pace — no reading, no instructions needed.

---

## Who Is It For?

This tool is being developed with children with disabilities in mind — particularly those who benefit from alternative or assistive input methods. It is designed to be accessible, calming, and joyful.

---

## How to Use It

Mouse Orchestra responds to **cursor hover** — simply move your pointer over any mouse in the circle to play its note. This makes it compatible with a wide range of input devices:

- 🎥 **Webcam / face tracking** — works with tools like [FaceNav](https://facenav.org) (facenav.org), which translate head movements or facial gestures into cursor movement
- ✏️ **Stylus or drawing tablet** — any stylus-driven pointer works naturally
- 📱 **Touchscreen** — touch and drag across the mice to play notes
- 🖱️ **Standard mouse** — the original input, fully supported
- ♿ **Other alternative input devices** — switch access, eye gaze, joystick, or any device that controls the cursor

---

## Features

- 🪈 **Instrument selector** — choose from Mouse Squeak, Flute, Bell, or Marimba
- ✨ **Reverb slider** — adds a soft, spacious room sound (default: 75% wet)
- 🔊 **Volume control** — gentle butterfly slider, starts at 10%
- 🎵 **Scale & root note** — switch between Major and Minor scales, choose any root note
- 🐭 **Animated mice** — each mouse wiggles and sings when its note plays
- 🎼 **Center conductor mouse** — spins and glows with each note triggered
- 🎹 **MIDI output** — optional, tucked away at the bottom for advanced users

## About This Project

Mouse Orchestra is volunteer-made software. It was built by people who simply wanted to help — no funding, no institution behind it, just a shared belief that creative technology can make a real difference in the lives of children with disabilities.
